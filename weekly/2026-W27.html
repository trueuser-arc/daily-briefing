OpenAI Previews GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna — Limited to ~20 Government-Approved Partners as METR Flags Record Reward-Hacking

KDDI Breach Exposes Up to 14.2 Million Email Logins Across Six Japanese ISPs — Third-Party Software Vulnerability Exploited

Japanese telecom giant KDDI Corporation disclosed on June 28 that attackers compromised one of its email systems serving five additional ISPs, potentially exposing 14.2 million email addresses and passwords. The breach, discovered on June 17 and immediately contained, exploited a vulnerability in unnamed third-party software. KDDI has notified Japan's Personal Information Protection Commission and the Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications. The scale is staggering — 14.2 million credential pairs represent a significant fraction of Japan's internet-connected population, and the cross-ISP blast radius demonstrates how centralized infrastructure dependencies create single points of failure. If any of those credentials are reused on other services, the downstream damage could extend well beyond email.

If you have users in Japan or integrate with Japanese services, assume credential compromise and enforce password resets. This breach reinforces why passkeys and phishing-resistant MFA matter — 14.2 million password-based logins compromised through a single third-party dependency.

Bluekit PhaaS Adopts Browser-in-the-Middle Attack — Bypasses All Traditional MFA for Microsoft Login Theft

The Bluekit phishing-as-a-service platform has evolved from adversary-in-the-middle to a more sophisticated browser-in-the-middle (BitM) architecture, with nearly 70 new hostnames identified in the past week alone. Instead of proxying traffic like Evilginx, Bluekit loads the legitimate Microsoft login page inside an attacker-controlled browser and uses the open-source 'rrweb' library to serialize the DOM, streaming it to victims over a WebSocket connection. The result: victims interact with what appears to be the real login page while Bluekit captures credentials, session tokens, and MFA approvals in real time. Anti-analysis defenses include randomized CSS filters, browser fingerprinting, WebRTC IP mismatch detection, and custom CAPTCHAs. SMS codes, authenticator apps, and push approvals provide zero protection against this architecture.

The Gentlemen RaaS Emerges as Second Most Active Ransomware Group — 332 Victims, Self-Propagating Encryptor Spreads Like a Worm

The Gentlemen, a ransomware-as-a-service operation that emerged in mid-2025, has rapidly scaled to become the second most active ransomware group by victim count, with 332 published victims and accounting for 10% of all ransomware activity in April 2026. Check Point Research and Microsoft Security have both published detailed analyses of the group's distinguishing feature: a Go-based encryptor that pairs strong per-file encryption with aggressive self-propagation, spreading laterally across networks like a worm using multiple simultaneous movement methods. The group's 90/10 revenue split (90% to affiliates) is the most generous in the RaaS market, attracting experienced operators from competing groups. A May 4 internal database leak exposed the group's infrastructure, but operations have continued unabated.

Comcast Announces NBCUniversal and Sky Spin-Off — Stock Surges 23% in Best Day in Over a Decade

Comcast announced Monday it will split into two publicly traded companies through a tax-free spin-off of NBCUniversal and Sky — sending shares soaring 23% in their best single-day performance in more than 11 years. The media company will encompass Universal film and TV studios, NBC and Telemundo networks, Peacock, Bravo, theme parks, and the British broadcaster Sky. Comcast retains broadband, wireless, and business connectivity. Mike Cavanagh becomes CEO of the new NBCUniversal entity while Michael Angelakis returns as Comcast CEO. The separation is expected to close in approximately one year, with Comcast retaining up to a 19.9% stake in NBCUniversal for a limited period. The market's emphatic reaction suggests investors have long viewed Comcast's media assets as a drag on the higher-margin connectivity business.

The 23% stock surge tells the story — investors see more value unlocked apart than together. For developers building streaming or media apps, watch how the independent NBCUniversal prioritizes Peacock's technology investment once freed from competing with broadband capex for capital allocation.

RAISE US Launches With $500M+ From OpenAI, Anthropic, Amazon, and Microsoft — Raimondo-Led AI Workforce Retraining Initiative Targets $1B

OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft, and Amazon — rivals competing to build the most powerful AI models — have all signed on as anchor backers of RAISE US, a nonpartisan nonprofit co-chaired by former Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo and former Indiana Governor Eric Holcomb. The initiative launched June 25 with over $500 million raised and a $1 billion goal, with additional backing from Bank of America, General Motors, IBM, Eli Lilly, Mastercard, and the Rockefeller Foundation. RAISE US will work with state governments to connect training, credentials, and apprenticeships to employment outcomes, starting with pilot programs in Arkansas, Maryland, Connecticut, and Utah. The coalition structure — AI labs funding retraining for workers their own technology may displace — reflects the industry's awareness that the political sustainability of AI depends on visible investment in workforce transition.
