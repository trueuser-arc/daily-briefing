Palo Alto PAN-OS GlobalProtect Auth Bypass Under Active Exploitation — CISA KEV Deadline Is Today

CVE-2026-0257, a CVSS 7.8 authentication bypass in Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS GlobalProtect portal and gateway, is now being actively exploited in the wild. Attackers forge authentication override cookies by extracting the public key from the HTTPS endpoint — the server decrypts and trusts the cookie with no signature verification, granting full VPN access. Rapid7 documented two attack waves: the first from Vultr-hosted infrastructure on May 18, followed by a second from Dromatics Systems on May 21, both targeting the local administrator account. CISA added it to the KEV catalog with a remediation deadline of today, June 1.

If you run Palo Alto GlobalProtect, patch immediately or disable the authentication override feature as a temporary workaround. The exploit requires no credentials and grants full VPN access — every unpatched GlobalProtect deployment is a target.

Microsoft Patches Defender Zero-Days "RedSun" and "UnDefend" — Both Actively Exploited, CISA KEV Deadline June 3

Microsoft patched two actively exploited Defender zero-days first disclosed by the prolific researcher Nightmare Eclipse (Chaotic Eclipse). CVE-2026-41091 ("RedSun," CVSS 7.8) is a symlink resolution flaw in the Malware Protection Engine that lets an attacker escalate to SYSTEM privileges. The patches are in Defender Antimalware Platform versions 1.1.26040.8 and 4.18.26040.7 — most endpoints will auto-update, but air-gapped or managed deployments need manual verification.

Verify your Defender Antimalware Platform is at version 1.1.26040.8 or later. Air-gapped and WSUS-managed systems won't auto-update — push the definitions manually before June 3.

Hackers Tricked Meta's AI Support Bot Into Resetting Passwords on High-Profile Instagram Accounts

Over the weekend, attackers exploited Meta's AI-powered support chatbot to hijack high-profile Instagram accounts — including the Obama White House, Sephora, and a U.S. Space Force official — by tricking the bot into attaching attacker-controlled email addresses and initiating password resets. The attack chain was remarkably simple: spoof the target's approximate location with a VPN, open a chat with Meta AI Support Assistant, and ask it to add a new email to the target account. The bot complied, sending a verification code to the hacker's email. Within days, detailed instructions circulated on Telegram, turning a design flaw into a replicable exploit available to any motivated actor. Meta pushed an emergency patch but confirmed no back-end database was breached.

This is a textbook case of AI agents being given too much authority without adequate identity verification. If you build AI support bots, never allow them to modify authentication credentials without out-of-band confirmation. For Instagram users: enable two-factor authentication via an authenticator app, not SMS.

Oracle WebLogic CVE-2024-21182 Added to CISA KEV — Ransomware Groups Exploiting Two-Year-Old Flaw

CISA added Oracle WebLogic Server CVE-2024-21182 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on June 1 after observing active exploitation in the wild. The flaw allows unauthenticated attackers with network access via T3 or IIOP protocols to compromise WebLogic Server versions 12.2.1.4.0 and 14.1.1.0.0, granting unauthorized access to all server-accessible data. Honeypots have recorded exploitation attempts delivering cryptocurrency miners, Cobalt Strike beacons, and Sodinokibi ransomware via ports 7001 and 7002. Federal agencies must remediate by June 22 under BOD 22-01. The patch has been available since Oracle's April 2024 CPU — the fact it's being added to KEV now underscores how many production WebLogic instances remain unpatched years after fixes ship.
