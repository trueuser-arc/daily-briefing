Ghost CMS SQL Injection Exploited in Large-Scale ClickFix Campaign — Harvard, Oxford, DuckDuckGo Among 700+ Victims

Attackers are chaining CVE-2026-26980, a critical SQL injection in Ghost CMS versions 3.24.0 through 6.19.0, with ClickFix social engineering to compromise over 700 domains including Harvard, Oxford, Auburn University, and DuckDuckGo. The attack extracts admin API keys via blind SQLi, then injects JavaScript that serves a fake Cloudflare verification prompt — tricking visitors into pasting a malicious command that drops DLL loaders, JavaScript droppers, or the Electron-based UtilifySetup.exe malware. A patch has existed since Ghost 6.19.1 (February 19), but the scale of this campaign underscores how many Ghost installations remain unpatched months later.

If you run Ghost CMS, update to 6.19.1+ immediately and audit your posts for injected JavaScript. If you visited a Ghost-powered site and encountered a Cloudflare verification prompt recently, scan your system for UtilifySetup.exe and related payloads.

Cisco SD-WAN Hit by Sixth Zero-Day of 2026 — CVSS 10.0 Auth Bypass Grants Full Admin via Single Packet

CVE-2026-20182 is a maximum-severity (CVSS 10.0) authentication bypass in Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Controller and Manager that lets a remote, unauthenticated attacker become an authenticated peer and inject SSH keys for the vmanage-admin account — all via a single crafted DTLS packet to UDP port 12346. The sophisticated threat actor UAT-8616 has been exploiting it in targeted attacks, then downgrading firmware to chain CVE-2022-20775 for root escalation before restoring the original version to hide their tracks. This is the sixth actively exploited Cisco SD-WAN zero-day in 2026, and CISA has added it to the KEV catalog with a three-day remediation deadline for federal agencies.

Organizations running Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN must apply the fixed release immediately — there is no workaround. The exploit is configuration-independent, meaning every deployment is vulnerable regardless of settings. Also check for signs of UAT-8616's firmware-downgrade technique in your environment.

"YellowKey" BitLocker Zero-Day Bypasses Encryption With a USB Stick and a Reboot — PoC Released

A researcher calling themselves "Nightmare Eclipse" dropped a zero-day exploit and proof-of-concept for CVE-2026-45585 (CVSS 6.8), which bypasses BitLocker full-disk encryption on Windows 11 and Server 2022/2025 using only native Windows functionality. The attack places specially crafted FsTx files on a USB drive, reboots the target into WinRE, and holds CTRL to spawn an unrestricted shell with full access to the encrypted volume — no specialized hardware, no credentials, no network access required. Microsoft has published manual mitigation guidance recommending users switch from TPM-only to TPM+PIN mode, which blocks the recovery-environment attack path entirely.

Apple Updating App Store Age Ratings in Australia and Vietnam Starting June 18 — Developers Must Verify Questionnaires Now

Apple announced that App Store age ratings in Australia and Vietnam will change on June 18. In Australia, the 15+ rating is being eliminated entirely — apps currently rated 15+ will be auto-bumped to 16+. In Vietnam, apps will receive region-specific ratings (00+, 12+, 16+, or 18+) based on App Store Connect questionnaire responses, aligning with Decree 147. This is part of a broader global regulatory wave around online safety for minors, and developers with apps relying on the 15+ category in Australia — particularly social, browser-based, and gaming apps — should review their questionnaire responses in App Store Connect before the cutoff.

EU Simplifies AI Act in Omnibus Deal — High-Risk AI Deadlines Pushed to December 2027, New Ban on AI-Generated CSAM

The EU Council and Parliament reached a provisional agreement on May 7 to streamline the AI Act as part of the "Omnibus VII" simplification package, with detailed analysis emerging through May 21. The most impactful change: standalone high-risk AI systems now have until December 2027 to comply (previously August 2026), and product-embedded high-risk AI gets until August 2028. The deal also adds a new outright prohibition on AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery and CSAM, tightens the AI Office's supervisory authority over general-purpose AI models, and extends simplified requirements to small and mid-cap companies. For developers shipping AI features into EU markets, this buys roughly 18 extra months of compliance runway.
