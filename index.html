CISA Issues BOD 26-04 — Rewrites Federal Patching Requirements With Risk-Based Framework and 3-Day Critical Deadlines

CISA issued Binding Operational Directive 26-04 on June 10, replacing both BOD 22-01 (the KEV catalog mandate from 2021) and BOD 19-02 (internet-facing vulnerability remediation from 2019) with a unified risk-based framework. Agencies must now assess four factors for every vulnerability: whether the asset is publicly exposed, whether the vulnerability is known to be exploited, whether exploitation is automatable, and whether exploitation grants partial or total control. If all four criteria are met, agencies have just three days to patch and must perform forensic triage to determine if systems were already compromised. Within 60 days, agencies must update their remediation processes, and within 180 days they must meet all remediation timelines. While the directive applies to federal civilian agencies, it will reset private-sector expectations too — every organization that benchmarks against CISA guidance now has a new standard for vulnerability prioritization.

Even if you're not a federal agency, BOD 26-04's four-factor risk framework is the clearest prioritization model CISA has ever published. Adopt it internally: publicly exposed + exploited + automatable + full control = patch within 72 hours, no exceptions.
