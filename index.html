Apache HTTP/2 Critical Double-Free Enables DoS and RCE — Update to 2.4.67 Immediately

CVE-2026-23918 (CVSS 8.8) is a double-free in Apache httpd 2.4.66's mod_http2 that triggers when a client sends an HTTP/2 HEADERS frame followed immediately by RST_STREAM before the multiplexer registers the stream. The DoS path is trivial — one TCP connection, two frames, no authentication — and researchers have built a working RCE proof of concept on x86_64 by placing a fake h2_stream struct at the freed address via mmap reuse. Apache 2.4.67 fixes the issue. Given httpd's ubiquity as a reverse proxy and web server, this affects a massive attack surface with no authentication required for exploitation.

Connecticut Passes Most Comprehensive State AI Law — Frontier Whistleblower Protections and Employment AI Rules Head to Governor

Connecticut's AI Responsibility and Transparency Act (SB 5) cleared the House 131-17 and Senate 32-4, making it the most sweeping state AI legislation in the country. It requires frontier model developers (training above 10²⁶ compute operations) to protect employees who report catastrophic risks and mandates anonymous internal reporting channels by January 2027. Separately, employers using AI as a "substantial factor" in hiring, promotion, or termination must disclose the technology's use and data sources to affected individuals. Governor Lamont has confirmed he will sign — a reversal from 2025 when he threatened a veto on similar legislation. The bill also creates a regulatory sandbox for companies testing new AI products under state oversight.

If you ship AI tools used in hiring or HR decisions, Connecticut's deployer obligations (effective October 2027) will require disclosure of your model's purpose, data categories, and sources to every affected employee and applicant.
