Check Point VPN Auth Bypass CVE-2026-50751 Exploited by Qilin Ransomware — CISA Gives Feds 3 Days to Patch

Check Point disclosed CVE-2026-50751 (CVSS 9.3) on Monday — a critical authentication bypass in Remote Access VPN, Mobile Access, and Spark Firewall products using the deprecated IKEv1 protocol. Attackers exploit a logic flaw in certificate validation during IKEv1 key exchange to establish VPN sessions without credentials. Active exploitation traces back to May 7 with a sharp surge over the weekend, and Check Point links at least one incident to a Qilin ransomware affiliate. CISA added it to the KEV catalog on June 8 with a remediation deadline of June 11 — just three days for federal agencies. A related vulnerability, CVE-2026-50752 (CVSS 7.4), was found in the same IKEv1 code path enabling MITM attacks against site-to-site tunnels.

If you run Check Point VPN with IKEv1, install the hotfix immediately or migrate to IKEv2. The attack requires no credentials and no interaction. Configurations that don't require machine certificates for connections are directly exploitable.
